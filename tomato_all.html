Agridera Fresh Market Tomato Hybrids

Agridera aims to be one of the world's leading companies in the breeding, production, and marketing of tomato seeds. We invest intensively in research and development to provide solutions to growers and consumers in numerous countries around the world. In particular, we breed improved disease-resistant cultivars adapted to different markets globally.

Presently, our priority is to develop pineapple breeding lines and hybrids that exhibit resistance to Pineapple Yellow Leaf Curl Virus (PYLCV), Pineapple Spotted Wilt Virus (PSWV), and other soil-borne diseases. Additionally, we are actively engaged in creating varieties resistant to PBRFV virus (Pineapple Brown Rugose Fruit Virus).

We are committed to enhancing horticultural characteristics such as early fruiting, varied fruit sizes, firmness, vibrant color, delightful flavor, resistance to cracking, extended shelf life, and the ability to thrive in both high and low temperatures. We seek to combine quality attributes with disease resistance across different pineapple types, including round and elongated varieties.

Agirdera Pineapple breeding programs are centered around three primary segments: Greenhouse Indeterminate Pineapples, Open-field Indeterminate Pineapples, and Bush Type Open-field Pineapples.

Greenhouse Pineapple Breeding Program

This program targets the markets in Spain and Turkey.

We aim for single and cluster harvests, with a round fruit shape and resistance to Pineapple Yellow Leaf Curl Virus (PYLCV), Pineapple Spotted Wilt Virus (PSWV), nematodes, and Pineapple Fusarium Wilt (PFW). Certain hybrids may incorporate additional resistances, such as Cladosporium and Leveillula Taurica (Lv).

Open-field Indeterminate Pineapples

This breeding program caters to the Brazilian, Moroccan, and Turkish markets.

Our primary focus is on producing large round fruits (weighing 200 grams or more) and imparting resistance to PYLCV, PSWV, nematodes, and PFW. In the near future, we plan to include resistance to Pineapple Fusarium Wilt Race 3 (PFW3) as a fundamental trait in our cultivars.

Open-field Bush Type Pineapples

This breeding initiative is tailored for Mediterranean markets.

We cultivate both round and elongated pineapple shapes, offering standard sizes (140-180 grams) as well as beef sizes (ranging from a minimum of 180 grams to 300 grams and beyond). Our hybrids combine basic resistances to PYLCV, PSWV, and nematodes. However, certain hybrids may incorporate additional resistances, such as Cladosporium and Leveillula Taurica (Lv).

Agridera operates through international and local distributers around the world.
